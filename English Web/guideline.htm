National Taiwan University Dining Services Committee Organization Regulations

Approved by the 1435th Administrative Meeting on Mar. 13, 1984.

Amended by the 1760th Administrative Meeting on Dec. 10, 1991.

Amended by the 1861st Administrative Meeting on Mar. 1, 1994.

Amended by the 1926th Administrative Meeting on Aug. 8, 1995.

Amended by the 1952nd Administrative Meeting on Mar. 5, 1996.

Amended by the 1967th Administrative Meeting on June 25, 1996.

Amended by the 1999th Administrative Meeting on Mar. 4, 1997.

Amended by the 2332nd Administrative Meeting on Mar. 9, 2004.

 

Article 1

In order to maintain a well-kept dining environment the Dining Services Committee (hereinafter referred to as the Committee) was formed by the University in accordance with the “Health and Hygiene Management Regulations for School and University Dining Halls, Kitchens, and Student Co-Ops” co-published by the Ministry of Education and the Department of Health.

Article 2

The Committee is responsible for the planning of the standards of establishment for the dining halls and co-ops of the University, the supervision of health and hygiene improvements, the reviews on management efficiencies, and the establishment of the relevant management laws and regulations.

Article 3

The Ex Officio members of the Committee are the President of the University, the Dean of General Affairs, the Dean of Student Affairs, the Comptroller of the Accounting Department, Director of Military Education Division, Representative of the Office of General Affairs, Chief Director of the Health Center, Director of the Biological Pollution Control Division, Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Center, Director of the Student Assistance Division, Director of the Student Housing Service Division, Director of Student Activity Center Administration Division, and the Director of Welfare Division, Student Association. Furthermore the President shall appoint 4 faculty members and 5 students as appointed members. In addition, the manager of student & staff co-op can be participated in this committee. Except for the manager, the terms of office for the aforesaid appointed members are one year and shall be extended upon being re-elected.

Article 4

The President of the University shall be the convener of the Committee where the Dean of General Affairs and the Dean of Student Affairs shall be the vice conveners and the Dean of General Affairs shall also be the Executive Secretary.

Article 5

The Committee shall call for one meeting every semester and provisional Meetings shall be called where necessary.

Article 6

The Committee is equipped with the following clerks who are appointed to the business directors of the Office of General Affairs and the Office of Student Affairs. All these positions are voluntary without pay and the responsibilities are listing hereunder :

 

1.

Administration Management Clerk:

Appointed to the Director of the Student Housing Service Division at the Office of Student Affairs, who is responsible for the convening of the Committee Meetings, documentation processing and safekeeping, and for providing assistance to the operation of the dining halls and co-ops of the University.

 

2.

Hygiene and Nutrition Clerk:

Appointed to the Chief Director of the Health Center at the Office of Student Affairs, who is responsible for the supervision and inspection on the hygiene and nutrition of the dining halls and co-ops of the University.

 

3.

General Affairs Clerk:

Appointed to the Director of General Services Division at the Office of General Affairs, who is responsible for the property loans and the signing of agreements concerning the dining halls and co-ops of the University.

 

4.

Construction Engineering Clerk:

Appointed to the Director of Construction and Maintenance Division at the Office of General Affairs, who is responsible for the design, repair, and maintenance affairs of the dining halls and co-ops of the University.

 

5.

Environmental Protection Clerk:

Appointed to the Director of the center appointed by the Director of the Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Center, who is responsible for the management of environmental hygiene of the dining halls and co-ops of the University, the prevention and treatment of air and waster pollution, and the management of waste products.

 

6.

Fire Safety Clerk :

Appointed to the clerk of the Protection Regiment, who is responsible for the inspections on the fire safety of the dining hall and co-op facilities of the University.

Article 7

A dining services execution team is formed under the Committee by the Construction and Maintenance Division of the Office of General Affairs, the Environmental Protection and Occupational Safety and Health Center, the Protection Regiment, Student Housing Service Division of the Office of Student Affairs, the Health Center, a faculty representative, and 2 student representatives. The Student Housing Service Division is responsible for the convention of the meetings, the implementation of non-scheduled inspections and examinations, the supervision of the dining hygiene, management, and facilities of the dining halls and co-ops of the University, and should submit the working report as well as improvement suggestions to the Committee.

Article 8

When meetings are held at the Committee relevant personnel shall be invited to be present at the meetings where approved by the convener.

Article 9

The Regulations are published for implementation after being approved by the Administrative Meeting and the same procedures shall apply to amendments which may arise.
