National Taiwan University Essentials for the Handling of Suspected Food Poisoning Cases

　

Content of Public Notice :

 

　

　

National Taiwan University Essentials for the Handling of Suspected Food Poisoning Cases

Approved by the 16th Dining Services Committee Meeting on Jan. 21, 1998.

Approved by the 2049th Administrative Meeting on Mar. 24, 1998.

Amended by the 2443rd Administrative Meeting on Aug. 1, 2006.

1.

Prevent the infection from spreading :

 

 

First report to the General-Duty Officer Room by telephone at 3366-9119 or the Campus Security on duty (24 hour service) at 3366-9110 following the procedures stated in the “Main Campus Emergency and Contingency Report System and Contacts” and the “Emergency and Contingency Processing Standards and Windows” of the University, and note the following items :

 

 

(1)

Where necessary transfer the patient to a hospital or clinic for examination and treatment and immediately notify the patient’s family or emergency contact person of the incident.

 

 

(2)

Personnel of the University in charge of processing the “Emergency and Contingency Processing Standards and Windows” or personnel from the Dining Services Committee should immediately report to the special line for food poisoning at the Department of Health of the Taipei City Government by phone 2720-5322 or by fax 2720-5321.

 

 

(3)

The Student Safety Division should be responsible in reporting the processing status to the Ministry of Education.

 

 

(4)

Assist and coordinate with the health organization of the district with the measures taken to prevent the incident from spreading.

2.

Investigate the cause of occurrence : 

 

 

(1)

Personnel from the Dining Services Committee should assist the district health organization in the execution of the investigations for the causes, the collection of samples and other relevant works.

 

 

(2)

After the district health organization has returned the investigation report and information the personnel from the Dining Services Committee should notify the Military Education Office and the unit responsible for the management of food and beverages to proceed with the follow-up works.

3.

Post-event handling processes :

 

 

(1)

All dining areas are to be sterilized fully and thoroughly.

 

 

(2)

Strengthen on the supervision of the health and safety of the areas where food and beverages are supplied.

 

 

(3)

Pay attention to the conditions of the patients.

 

 

(4)

Act in accordance with the instructions given by the district health organization after the investigations.
